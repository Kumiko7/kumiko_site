Summary (Click to reveal)

Janu arrives at the park, feeling as though he has been transported there suddenly. He encounters Mori, who is accompanied by her dog and appearing uncharacteristically happy. Mori dismisses the idea of a "happy" or "sad" version of herself, insisting there is only one Mori. As Janu questions her strange behavior and the fact that he does not have her phone number, she grows hostile and commands him to leave, causing the scene to reset.

After the reset, Mori shares a nostalgic story about a Christmas nearly a year prior, where she and Janu met in the park to watch the snow fall. She recounts how they held each other, wrote songs together, and discussed their plans, but mentions that the evening ended abruptly when Janu disappeared, which devastated her.

During the storytelling, Chinaka briefly appears and speaks with Roni, who is also at the park to clear her head. After Chinaka leaves, the scene returns to Mori and Janu. Mori finishes her recollection, revealing that she held Janu’s hand—noting that it felt unnaturally cold—before he vanished. She pleads with Janu not to disappear on her again if he has the power to prevent it, ending the encounter.
