After missing his train to Tokyo, Nokana's brother, Niji, encounters Janu near the bookstore. Realizing he has two hours to wait, Niji invites Janu to grab a drink. Janu reluctantly agrees, and the two men head to a local bar. During their conversation, Niji shares his existential perspective on life, discussing the nature of "free thinkers" and his past struggles with mental despair. He reveals that he has experienced a dark period of his own but managed to find hope.

The conversation shifts to Nokana and her personal growth. Niji expresses concern for his sister’s well-being and her current rift with her friend, Mori, noting Nokana’s lifelong tendency to isolate herself due to insecurities. He also discusses Nirima’s long-standing crush on him, acknowledging his own sexuality and expressing a desire for Janu to help navigate Nirima's feelings without hurting her. Niji ultimately praises Janu’s positive influence on Nokana and asks him to treat her gently as her feelings for him deepen. The event concludes with Niji heading to the metro station to catch his train, leaving Janu to reflect on the encounter and his growing relationship with Nokana.
