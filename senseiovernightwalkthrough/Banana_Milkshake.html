Janu finds Juna in the dormitory area, struggling with a faulty vending machine and expressing frustration over losing a thousand yen. Their interaction highlights their unique bond as clones; Juna laments the lack of control they have over their existence and their inability to stay in the world indefinitely. The conversation shifts toward their family dynamic, specifically touching upon the self-sufficiency of Dayai.

Juna eventually convinces Janu to accompany her to a convenience store, with Janu acting as her financial support. On the bus and at the store, they share an awkward but rare moment of connection outside their shared apartment. They encounter an acquaintance who mentions a help-wanted sign, leading Juna to consider getting a job to alleviate her financial dependence on Janu. The outing is punctuated by a video call from Dayai, who checks on their progress and asks them to return home for dinner. The scene concludes with the two returning home with groceries, where they share a meal of omurice prepared by Dayai, marking a peaceful and domestic conclusion to their excursion.
