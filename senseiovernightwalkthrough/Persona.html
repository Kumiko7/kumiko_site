Summary (Click to reveal)

Janu visits Roni in the courtyard to kill time. During their conversation, Roni expresses surprise at Janu’s interest in spending time with her, leading to a vulnerable moment where Janu admits he feels intimidated by his students. This confession surprises Roni and encourages her to open up about her history, including her past as a bully and her previous negative experiences with other teachers at the school. She shares how she struggled to fit in and often clashed with authority figures, leading her to adopt a "tough" persona that she is now trying to shed.

Their conversation is interrupted by the arrival of Sora, who asks Janu to act as the banker for a game of Monopoly. Roni’s presence triggers a hostile reaction from Sora, who refuses to play if Roni is involved, citing Roni's past bullying. The tension escalates as Roni confronts Sora about her lingering fear and prejudice.

Janu is then presented with a choice: - If he intervenes, he tells Roni to stop scaring Sora. Roni lashes out at Sora’s hypocrisy, and Janu expresses his disappointment in Sora for her behavior, leaving her to reflect on her actions while Roni departs to be alone. - If he stays out of it, Roni aggressively challenges Sora to admit that she will always view her as a bully. Sora is left terrified and begging for the harassment to stop. Roni eventually relents, realizing Sora isn't worth her energy, and leaves to find a place to vent her frustration.

In both outcomes, the event concludes with Janu reflecting on the intensity of the encounter and the realization that both students are struggling with their own pain.
