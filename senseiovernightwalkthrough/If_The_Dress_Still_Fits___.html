Summary (Click to reveal)

Janu visits D’vala at the art gallery, finding her struggling with the workplace’s technology while under pressure from her boss. After her boss leaves, Janu jokes about attempting to sneak up on her. During their conversation, D’vala mentions that Juna, Janu’s niece, has been encouraging her to stay away from him. Janu explains to D’vala that his distance stems from the difficulty of reconciling her current appearance with his memories of their past, though he admits to feeling drawn to her.

Janu warns D’vala to be discreet about their relationship to avoid trouble, given his position as a teacher. Despite this, D’vala maintains an affectionate demeanor. After discussing the gallery’s art, D’vala insists on showing Janu a "special thing" in the storage room. There, she reveals a vintage gown Janu once bought her, which she discovered at a local thrift store. Moved by the gesture, Janu reflects on their past, and the two share a vulnerable moment where D’vala admits her lingering feelings and her desire to start over. The scene concludes after they exchange phone numbers, with Janu later receiving an apology from Juna at his home, suggesting a tentative, albeit tense, reconciliation.
