Summary (Click to reveal)

Janu initiates a tutoring session with Nirima to investigate why she continues to attend despite her academic aptitude and apparent lack of focus. During the meeting, their dialogue is tense and flirtatious; Nirima calls out Janu for staring at her chest, while Janu defends his interest in her academic performance. Nirima reveals that she stopped prioritizing her classwork because Janu’s own unorthodox, "anti-establishment" teaching methods caused her to lose interest in the school's standard requirements.

Nirima clarifies that she is not actually struggling, but rather performing apathy to avoid being labeled a "nerd." Their conversation shifts to the school’s social dynamics and Nirima’s role as a mentor for younger students, specifically referencing her close bond with Anii. Ultimately, Janu acknowledges that Nirima no longer requires tutoring, and he officially releases her from the sessions.

The scene concludes as Nirima and Janu head to the Film club, where they encounter a group of students—Yuka, Rei, Aisha, and Hana. The girls discuss their upcoming assignment and debate the necessity of club activities. Nirima introduces Janu to the students, maintaining her sharp, defensive wit, while Janu reflects on his shift from teacher to an observer of Nirima's new responsibilities.
