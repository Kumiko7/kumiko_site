Summary (Click to reveal)

Janu awakens in a pristine, unoccupied hospital room accompanied by a brooding melody he finds unsettling. He explores the room, checking various beds and interacting with objects—including a series of student figurines that he handles in an intimate, invasive manner—before finally being able to interact with the door.

Upon opening the door, Janu encounters Valda, a "spiritual consultant" who explains that they are in "The Gateway," a spiritual plane where souls arrive following death. Valda reveals that Janu is a unique case: he is able to leave and return to his physical body at will. Valda identifies as a version of someone Janu knew who died by suicide, and she reveals that Janu is a pivotal figure—the "first version"—whose death marks the end of his world.

A volatile confrontation ensues between Janu and a mysterious entity (the narrator/conductor) who has been influencing his life and inhabiting his body. The entity accuses Janu of lacking a spine and being passive, while Janu expresses a desire to live his own life and be a better man. The entity pressures Janu to acknowledge his power, eventually forcing him to admit that he is the sole conductor of his universe. The scene concludes with Janu returning to his physical reality, feeling burdened by his newfound knowledge and the realization that his relationships with his students may be influenced by these metaphysical forces. The event ends as Janu prepares to face the consequences of his existence.
